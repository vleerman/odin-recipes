Dashi

Description

Dashi is a japanese stock used for many japanese dishes. We will describe the recipe for konbudashi which uses dried kelp and bonito flakes. The stock will have a great umami flavor!

Ingredients

  • 1 ounce dashi kombu (dried kelp)
  • 1 quart water
  • 1/2 cup bonito flakes

Steps

  1. Place the kombu and water in a saucepan, allow it to soak for 30 minutes
  2. Remove the kombu from the water, cut lenghtwise slits. Return the kombu to the water and bring to a boil. When it boils, remove the kombu to prevent the stock from becoming bitter
  3. Stir the bonito flakes into the water, bring back to a boil. Once it does, take the pan off the heat. Allow water to cool. Strain the dashi and you are done!
